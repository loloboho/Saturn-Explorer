================================================================================
DELTA-V CALCULATION
================================================================================

Rocket Equation: Δv = Isp × g₀ × ln(m₀/m_f)

Given:
• Isp = 15,000 seconds
• g₀ = 9.81 m/s²
• m₀ (loaded) = 4,418.7 tonnes
• m_f (dry) = 2,366.7 tonnes
• Mass ratio = 4,418.7 / 2,366.7 = 1.867

Calculation:
Δv = 15,000 × 9.81 × ln(1.867)
Δv = 147,150 × 0.624
Δv = 91,821 m/s

TOTAL DELTA-V CAPABILITY: 91.8 km/s

THRUST & ACCELERATION PERFORMANCE
================================================================================

Exhaust Velocity (Ve):
Ve = Isp × g₀ = 15,000 × 9.81 = 147,150 m/s

Thrust Calculation:
With 73 MW thrust power:
Thrust = 2 × Power / Ve
Thrust = 2 × 73,000,000 W / 147,150 m/s
Thrust ≈ 992 N ≈ 1.0 kN

Acceleration Profile:

At Departure (Loaded):
• Mass: 4,418.7 tonnes = 4,418,700 kg
• Thrust: 1,000 N
• Acceleration: 1,000 / 4,418,700 = 0.000226 m/s²
• In milligravs: 0.023 millig

At Arrival (Dry):
• Mass: 2,366.7 tonnes = 2,366,700 kg
• Thrust: 1,000 N
• Acceleration: 1,000 / 2,366,700 = 0.000423 m/s²
• In milligravs: 0.043 millig

Average Mission Acceleration: ~0.033 millig

Performance Summary:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Configuration    Mass (t)    Thrust (kN)    Accel (millig)    Accel (m/s²)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Loaded (dep.)    4,418.7     1.0            0.023             0.000226
Mid-mission      3,400       1.0            0.029             0.000287
Dry (arrival)    2,366.7     1.0            0.043             0.000423
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Operational Implications:
• Very low thrust = Long burn times (months)
• Gentle acceleration = Comfortable for crew
• Continuous thrust = No ballistic coasting phases
• Variable gravity available throughout (ring rotation)

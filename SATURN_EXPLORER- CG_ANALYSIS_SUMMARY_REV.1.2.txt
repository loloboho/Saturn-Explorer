================================================================================
DESIGN RATIONALE - REV 1.2 CHANGES
================================================================================

Radiator Position (STA 119.5):
✓ Closer to reactor (102.6) for thermal efficiency
✓ Shorter piping runs, reduced heat loss
✓ Between reactor and nozzles - good packaging
✓ Minimal CG penalty vs optimal position
✓ Practical engineering over pure optimization

Nozzle Position (STA 125.0):
✓ Moved aft for better weight distribution
✓ Improved thrust alignment with CG
✓ Clearance from radiators
✓ Good structural attachment point
✓ Reasonable distance from ship end (140.8)

Combined Effect:
✓ CG shift 1.90m (26% margin below 2.0m limit)
✓ Thermally sensible configuration
✓ Maintainable component spacing
✓ Buildable with good access

within acceptable limits and provides adequate margin for operational safety.

Assembly Sequence (Orbital Construction):

2. Square tubing "squirrel cage" framework

Phase 3 - Complete Sphere:
1. Forward hemisphere sections
2. Whipple cap (STA 0.0-3.8)
3. Sphere sealed - 2m diameter airlock only access

Phase 4 - Final Outfitting:
1. Interior partition panels (hook & loop, movable)
2. Small equipment through airlock
3. Crew provisions and supplies

Critical Constraint:
- All components >2m diameter must be installed before sphere completion
- Ring (26m dia), Shelter (9m dia), and major equipment go in early
- 2m airlock limits post-closure installations

Maintenance Access:
- Utilities on exterior ring surface
- Workers rappel from Deck 3 in variable gravity
- Ring rotation provides "down" direction for easier work

================================================================================
THERMAL SYSTEM OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Habitat Heating:
- Primary: Electric IR emitters on sphere inner surface
- Maintains 20°C throughout sphere
- Sphere interior: Low-emissive silver coating (reduces heat loss)
- Ring: Warmed by sphere radiation + small zone heaters as needed

Domestic Hot Water (DHW):
- Point-of-use electric on-demand heaters
- Located at galley, showers, sinks
- No long pipe runs (100m to reactor too far)
- Instant hot water, no storage tanks

Heat Rejection:
- Reactor waste heat → Radiators @ STA 119.5
- Radiators: 11t, positioned near reactor for efficiency
- Slanted configuration (future structural detail)
- Excess heat radiated to space

Power Source:
- D-T fusion reactor @ STA 102.6
- High-voltage DC cables (3-5 kV) to sphere
- ~70m cable run with step-up/step-down transformers
- Reliable, proven technology (vs microwave transmission)

Design Philosophy:
- Electric heating: Simple, reliable, maintainable
- No complex hydronic loops over long distances
- Heat exactly where and when needed
- Proven technology scaled for spacecraft use
